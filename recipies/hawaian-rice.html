Hawaian Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • Ham: Sometimes, to save on time, I buy the ham at the store that is already cubed and ready to go!
  • White Onion: Make sure that you are cutting up your vegetables to be similar in size so that they all cook at the same rate.
  • Red Bell Pepper: Seed and chop this up to mix into the rice.
  • Eggs: I like to use large eggs whenever I am cooking in the kitchen
  • Rice: Cook your rice beforehand. Actually, day-old rice mixes in the best for this recipe! I like to use jasmine rice.
  • Pineapple: You can use fresh pineapple, or you can use a can of them to make things easier.
  • Sesame oil: This adds THE best flavor to the rice.
  • Green Onions: These are an optional garnish that I like to put on top, but I wouldnâ€™t skip them! They add in a lot of color and flavor.

Steps:

  1. Cook veggies Preheat a large skillet or wok to medium heat.
    Pour vegetable oil in the bottom. Add chopped ham, white onion, and red bell pepper and fry until tender.
  2. Cook the Eggs Slide the ham, onion, and red bell pepper to the side,
    and pour the beaten eggs onto the other side. Using a spatula, scramble the eggs. Once cooked, mix the eggs with the vegetable mix.
  3. Combine cheese mixture Add the rice and pineapple to the veggie, ham and egg mixture.
    Pour the soy sauce and sesame oil on top. Stir and fry the rice mixture and veggie mixture until heated through and combined. Add chopped green onions if desired
